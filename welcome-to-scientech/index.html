Welcome to ScienTech

Saturday, 30 January 2021 Engineering

Bem-vindo! Welcome! Welkom! Bienvenu!

Hey, welcome to ScienTech, I'm Paulo, main author of this blog and I'm glad you joined me in this project. For me, sharing knowledge is a privilege and I hope we can grow together through the articles in here.

ScienTech is and will be a place for you to discover interesting ideas, tools and concepts with a focused perspective over the core, over the why of that, thriving to be concise and enjoyable for you to read and learn. I will write over things I wish I knew before, regarding the engineering aspects and its applications, things I learned the hard way and were not available in the books and over major science / technology topics, from an engineer point of view, in a very practical and applicable way, as I wanted. I will share tools, skills and notions I collected over my experiences that will sure help you.

Many ideas are in my mind and I would be happy to receive suggestions over what I should write about. As the name states, the main focus of this blog is to talk about science and technology, from a perspective of a engineer, transforming that dense and sometimes boring writing into something that is easy to read. But my main goal is to study and learn about topics that makes you grow and not only engineering topics.  Personal skills and others as well get my attention easily because they are practical and necessary for growth, so fell free to point out interesting themes outside of the 'scientech' sphere as well, pointing out what you liked and made you grow. Some of the series that I plan to share here are:

  • TLDR - Too long didn't read: Are a series of small posts with the core concept of engineering, a direction in where you could find more about that or a useful tool that helped me solve issues in real life or improved my performance in my work or life projects. 

 

  • YSK - You should know: As an engineer I should be able to solve problems with a long and detailed knowledge over the subject but as well on the spot with minimum information. Some necessary skill to deal with it are not mathematical but more human related than anything else. I will also talk over some people skills I had to develop to get of problematic situations and also some very cool software's that saved me more than once.

 

  • Toolbox: I will suggest and discuss in more depth over a tools (computational/mathematical and more) that helped me or are in overall a good one for you have in mind to be more prepared. Also on how you can you substitute a private tool with a open source one.

These are just the ideas from a first brain storm and I hope to come up with more that will enhance you personally and professionally. 

One of the best figure of speech and the one I like the most, I learned about it as a "nickname", the jack-of-all-trades, but later I learned that the story of this nickname that goes beyond, it is similar to a poem, one that I believe is critical for the engineer and to anyone who thrives to be better. The quote goes as : 

Jack of all trades
Master of none
Though oftentimes better
Than master of one 

Hope I can keep summarizing and sharing knowledge in a consistent schedule, the goal is to publish at least one article a week, so stay tuned.

A RSS feed will be implemented in the near future as well so you can get notice of the post as well as a newsletter.

Thank you for staying and hope we can grow together through the challenges of life.

Ad Studium, Ad Communicant Et Ad Crescendum.
